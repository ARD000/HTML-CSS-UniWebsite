Hejlsberg’s Early Life

Anders Hejlsberg was born 2nd December 1960 in Denmark.
He studied Electrical Engineering at the Technical University of Denmark.
He wrote programs for the Nascom Microcumputer, inluding a Blue Label Software Pascal for the Nascom-2. This is essentially a Pascal Compiler, a program that converts instructions into a machine code meant to be analysed, read and, excuted by a computer.

Hejlsberg’s Mid Life

Anders Hejlsberg moved to California in 1989 to work as a Chief Engineer at Borland.
He was the lead architect in the team that developed Borland Delphi, a general purpose programming language.
He joined Microsoft in 1996 and created J++ while becoming a Technical Fellow and a Microsoft Distinguished Engineer.
Finally in 2000 he started the development of C# as the Lead Architect.
Recently in 2012 he announced a new project known as TypeScript a superset of JavaScript, it is a free and open source programming language.
